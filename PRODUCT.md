# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Agency owners and account managers running social media operations across multiple clients. They open Loudmouth HQ mid-workflow: checking calendar status for a client, reviewing content plans before a call, delegating tasks to SMMs and designers, managing approvals. Secondary users are the SMMs and graphic designers they manage — working inside calendars and grids, not overseeing the whole system.

The primary user is competent, time-pressured, and has zero patience for UI that slows them down. They're used to being the most decisive person in any room.

## Product Purpose

Loudmouth HQ is a social media operations platform for agencies. It replaces the spreadsheet-and-Slack stack with structured workflows: calendar creation, content planning, client portals, scheduling, billing, and team RBAC. Success means an agency owner can see exactly what's happening across all clients at a glance and act on it without hunting through tools.

## Brand Personality

Loud and unapologetic. The name means something. This is a tool built for people who hate boring tools — big type, strong contrast, committed color choices. Not edgy for edge's sake; confident because the users are confident. Three words: bold, direct, capable.

Voice and tone: terse and declarative. No filler copy. Labels are commands, not suggestions. Error messages tell you what happened and what to do next, not what might possibly have occurred.

## Anti-references

- **Enterprise grey**: muted greys, small type, dense unreadable tables, legacy B2B energy (Salesforce, Jira). This is the opposite of what Loudmouth should feel.
- **Minimalist Swiss**: ultra-restrained white space, quiet typography, correct-but-joyless. The tool has opinions and shows them.

## Design Principles

1. **Nothing subtle.** If you have to look twice to notice it, it should be bolder or removed. Loudmouth doesn't whisper.
2. **Confidence over comfort.** The interface has a point of view. It doesn't hedge with soft shadows and rounded cards — it commits.
3. **Tools for doers.** Every element earns its place through function. Decoration that doesn't aid comprehension or action gets cut.
4. **Own the room.** The users run agencies. The tool should match their energy: fast to navigate, instant to understand, no hand-holding.
5. **Dark by conviction.** The dark theme is a deliberate choice for a high-contrast, high-energy working environment — not a trend.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort: avoid obvious failures (low contrast text, missing form labels, broken keyboard nav). The existing chartreuse-on-dark palette is high contrast by nature. No formal WCAG compliance target.
